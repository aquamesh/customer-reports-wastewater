Example Case Study

High Load Wastewater Monitoring

At a representative discharge site, the operator's main problem was delayed visibility: grab samples could miss short spikes, fixed sensors could struggle with turbidity and color interference, and fouling created uncertainty around data quality. For this type of site, AquaMesh is configured before installation around the expected pollution load, calibration scale, adjustable optical path length, maintenance access, power availability, and communications needs.

Deployment approach

  • Configured for continuous wastewater monitoring.
  • Uses absorbance, fluorescence, turbidity, temperature, and camera-based site inspection.
  • Supports organic load, nitrate-related absorbance, contamination fluorescence, turbidity, and oil or hydrocarbon indicators.
  • Includes tunable compensation for temperature, ambient light, turbidity, and inner filter effects.

Heavy pollution configuration

  • Adjustable optical path length helps keep signals inside the usable measurement window.
  • Internal scale calibration is set around the operator's target concentration range.
  • Configuration reduces saturation risk in heavily polluted water.
  • Preserves useful resolution for operational decision-making.

Operation

  • Supports continuous, scheduled, or event-triggered monitoring.
  • High-risk discharge points can prioritize frequent readings and rapid alerts.
  • Configured indicators can include turbidity, organic load, and fluorescence patterns.
  • Lower-risk remote sites can reduce sampling frequency to extend autonomy.

Maintenance plan

  • Assumes fouling pressure from solids, grease, biofilm, algae, and variable flow.
  • Self-cleaning wiper, UV antifouling LEDs, and antifoul coating can reduce avoidable visits.
  • Onboard camera inspection helps verify when cleaning is actually needed.
  • Maintenance shifts from fixed visits toward condition-informed service.

Autonomy and power

  • Mesh networking supports distributed wastewater monitoring.
  • Local storage and gateway upload support remote infrastructure.
  • Battery backup and solar-capable deployment support sites without reliable power.
  • Integrates with third-party pH, TSS, DO, and flow sensors.

Resulting value

  • 5x broader site coverage at the same equipment budget.
  • Minutes instead of days for networked monitoring setup.
  • Continuous monitoring instead of periodic grab samples.
  • Fewer separate instruments per location.
  • Lower maintenance uncertainty through camera inspection and condition-informed service.
